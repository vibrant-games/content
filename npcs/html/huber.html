Ghall'Shaarat
At 3rd level, you learn to imbue a weapon with a fraction of your spirit. The Dhakaani daashors called this weapon the ghaal’shaarat, Goblin for “mighty blade.” Imbuing your ghaal’shaarat works like infusing an item, but does not count toward your maximum number of infused items. When you finish a long rest, you can touch a magic or nonmagical simple or martial melee weapon, which becomes your ghaal’shaarat. Once imbued, your ghaal’shaarat is a magic weapon that grants a +1 bonus to attack and damage rolls, unless the base weapon you imbued already grants a higher bonus. This bonus increases when you reach certain levels in this class, increasing to +2 at 8th level and +3 at 13th level. If the weapon you imbued has the thrown property, it returns to your hand immediately after it is used to make a ranged attack. Your ghaal’shaarat’s benefits remain until the weapon is destroyed or until you use this ability to imbue a new item with this power. Other creatures can’t benefit from your ghaal’shaarat’s powers, and to them, it functions as the unimbued weapon did.

Background Story

 Huber grew up in a clan that were former slaves and currently closely related to the naturally tinkering Rock Gnomes. The Gremlin Clan are different than other goblins because of their relationship with the gnomes. Huber grew up jealous of his felow gremlins who have a natural affinity for artifice and creating. Many of his clan's members have been invited to work with the Collegium Imaginata in various capacities, having asserted his jealous dominance within his clan, he was brought in to supervise the Collegium's retinue of goblin lackeys. He leads with more anger and bluster than actual efficiency

Personality

 Huber is large for his clan but not as intelligent as most of relatives. What he lacks in brains, he more than makes up for in rage and dominance. He has the same red skin, pointy nose and pointy ears that is the halmark of his clan. He dresses in coveralls and wears a yellow hat that end in a point that he thinks denotes his role as leader. He is jealous of any accomplishments that his clan members make and will always do his best to take credit for their work. Most of his clan hates him, but are too afraid of him to not follow his orders.
